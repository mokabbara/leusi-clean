Tripoli field action
An unsafe situation was spotted, escalated and addressed

While in Tripoli, I identified a serious unsafe condition at an active urban construction site. I contacted project stakeholders quickly and introduced them to the free LEUSI AI Safety Agent as a practical awareness and risk-identification tool.

The issue was taken seriously and resolved constructively. This became a real proof of concept: LEUSI can support practical risk communication and early intervention before incidents occur.
Solution after intervention
Visible site controls were strengthened after the issue was raised

Following the quick escalation, the site response included clearer perimeter protection, stronger visual warning signs, restricted-entry messaging, PPE requirement boards, emergency contact information, and better visible separation between the public area and the excavation zone.

✓Deep excavation and unauthorised-entry warnings made visible in English and Arabic.
✓PPE requirements displayed at the site boundary, including helmet, vest, boots and gloves.
✓Site information board and emergency contacts placed where workers and visitors can see them.
✓The case shows how early observation, direct communication and practical safety guidance can lead to rapid improvement.

Note: LEUSI supports awareness and risk communication. Formal compliance remains the responsibility of the project owner, contractor and competent HSSE professionals.
Strategic social investment conversation
LEUSI presented to Shell global social investment leadership

LEUSI was presented to Sreejeeta Darta, Shell Global Social Investment Manager, as a practical safety-awareness and worker-readiness initiative that can connect European HSSE standards with local realities in fragile and underserved markets.

The discussion opened a strategic question: how could LEUSI support future social investment thinking in potential markets and communities not yet strongly represented on Shell’s current map, including Lebanon and Syria, where worker safety, public trust, and institutional readiness remain urgent needs.

Note: this is an early strategic conversation and not a formal funding commitment or partnership announcement. Stichting registration remains necessary before LEUSI can engage institutions through formal channels.

●LEUSI can act as a bridge between global safety expectations and local worker-facing awareness.
●The Stichting registration is needed before LEUSI can engage formal partners, receive support transparently, and structure future social investment pathways.
●This is an early conversation, not a formal partnership or funding commitment at this stage.
