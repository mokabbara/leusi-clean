Founder note
From awareness to responsibility
I am grateful for the institutional encouragement that has helped LEUSI reach this point. We are now building relationships with local constituencies, institutions and potential partners who can translate purpose into practical impact for frontline workers.

During my recent visit to Lebanon, I engaged with the Vice President of the Tripoli Municipal Council, Mr. Khaled Kabbara, and prepared an initial contribution of PPE suits and safety helmets as a modest but symbolic first step. Safety starts with visibility, responsibility and the recognition that every worker deserves protection, dignity and practical support.

LEUSI was also presented to stakeholders connected to the Port of Tripoli, represented by Mr. Nour Alameddine. The Port has shown strong interest in raising HSSE awareness among frontline workers and local contractors. In addition, engagement with the Tripoli Chamber of Commerce and Mr. Tawfik Dabbousi opened a pathway toward partnerships with local foundations and economic actors.
First fundraising dinner
Community, food, art and a practical founding step.
In collaboration with Kunst Bar in Noordeinde, The Hague, LEUSI organized its first fundraising dinner and raised €500 toward the notary costs. The evening showed that LEUSI can gather people around a shared mission: safer work, worker dignity, and a bridge between European safety standards and local realities in the Levant.
Tripoli field action
Unsafe situation spotted, escalated and addressed.
While in Tripoli, I spotted an unsafe urban construction situation with visible excavation exposure, public interface risks and weak safety separation. I contacted the project stakeholders quickly and introduced the free LEUSI AI Safety Agent as a practical multilingual awareness and photo-risk assessment tool.
Initial situation observed
Visible site controls improved after response
The response showed openness to rapid action, clearer communication, bilingual danger signage, PPE boards, improved separation, and stronger visual safety controls. Formal compliance remains with the contractor and project owner, while LEUSI’s role is to support awareness, coaching and early risk communication.
Public awareness and AI tool
First Facebook safety awareness campaign and LEUSI AI demonstration.
LEUSI launched its first Facebook awareness campaign around a recent building-collapse accident during demolition. The campaign explained visible hazards, urgent corrective actions, PPE needs and worker coaching points.

Strategic social investment conversation
LEUSI presented to Shell global social investment leadership.
LEUSI was introduced to Sreejeeta Darta, Shell Global Social Investment Manager, as a potential social-impact platform connecting European HSSE standards with local realities in markets such as Lebanon and Syria, where safety awareness, worker readiness and community resilience remain urgent needs.

This is an early strategic conversation and not a formal partnership or funding commitment. It does, however, reinforce why Stichting registration and formal governance are now urgent: LEUSI needs the legal structure required to engage responsibly with institutional partners.
Regional pathway
Opening a door toward Saudi Arabia and wider regional construction markets.
The Tripoli project stakeholders are connected to a major real estate and construction network active in Lebanon and Saudi Arabia. Their interest in the free LEUSI AI Safety Agent may open future opportunities for LEUSI in new regions where contractor safety culture, VCA-inspired readiness and worker-facing training are increasingly relevant.
